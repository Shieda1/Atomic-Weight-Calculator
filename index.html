What is the atomic weight?
The atomic weight is the average mass of atoms of an element. In other words the atomic weight is the weighted average of the masses of naturally-occurring isotopes.
How to calculate atomic weight?
To calculate atomic weight you should use the following formula:
atomic weight = (abundance of isotope 1 / 100) * mass of isotope 1 + (abundance of isotope 2 / 100) * mass of isotope 2 + .....
Example: The abundance of carbon-12 is 98.89% and it's mass is 12, the abundance of carbon-13 is 1.11% and it's mass is 13.0034, calculate the atomic weight?
Using the atomic weight formula the answer will be:
atomic weight = (abundance of isotope 1 / 100) * mass of isotope 1 + (abundance of isotope 2 / 100) * mass of isotope 2 + .....
atomic weight = (98.89 / 100) * 12 + (1.11 / 100) * 13.0034
atomic weight = 12.0111
Why use atomic weight?
The atomic weight is used to find the average mass of molecules and elements and also to solve stochiometry problems.
